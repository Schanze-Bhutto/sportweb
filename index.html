FOOTBALL

WHAT IS FOOTBALL?

A global team sport played by eleven players, aiming to score goals by kicking or heading the ball. It demands strategic play, endurance, and skill, fostering unity and passion worldwide. It's the world's most popular game.

HOW MANY PLAYERS PLAY THIS GAME?

Each team consists of 11 players on the field, including a goalkeeper.

STRATEGY

Football strategies involve tactical formations (e.g., 4-4-2, 4-3-3), possession play, counter-attacking, set-piece execution, and defensive organization. Team strategy varies based on opponent strength and game situation.

IS IT A TEAM SPORT?

Yes, football is a team sport that requires coordination and cooperation among players to achieve victory.

RULES OF THE GAME

The game is played with a spherical ball between two teams of 11 players. The objective is to score by kicking or heading the ball into the opposing team's goal. Players cannot use their hands or arms except for the goalkeeper within their penalty area. Offside rules, fouls, and penalties govern play.

IMPORTANT FACTS ABOUT THE GAME

  • Football's modern rules were codified in England in the 19th century.
  • The FIFA World Cup is the most prestigious international football tournament, held every four years.
  • Football is the world's most popular sport.
  • The first official international football match was played in 1872 between England and Scotland.

STATISTICS/RECORDS

Records include most World Cup titles (Brazil), most goals scored in a career (Cristiano Ronaldo), most Ballon d'Or awards (Lionel Messi), and fastest goal scored. Many other team and individual records exist.

JUDO

WHAT IS JUDO?

Judo is a modern martial art and combat sport created in Japan. It emphasizes throws and grappling, aiming to subdue opponents with minimal striking.

HOW MANY PLAYERS PLAY THIS GAME?

Judo is typically a one-on-one combat sport.

STRATEGY

Judo strategy focuses on throws, holds, and submissions, using an opponent's momentum and balance against them.

IS IT A TEAM SPORT?

No, judo is an individual sport.

RULES OF THE GAME

Judo matches involve throwing an opponent to their back, pinning them, or forcing a submission. Penalties are given for illegal moves.

IMPORTANT FACTS ABOUT THE GAME

  • Judo means "gentle way."
  • It was created by Jigoro Kano in 1882.
  • Judo is an Olympic sport.

STATISTICS/RECORDS

Records include Olympic and World Championship titles, and individual win-loss ratios.

COUNTRIES THAT PLAY THIS GAME

Japan
France
Brazil
South Korea
Netherlands
Russia

TENNIS

WHAT IS TENNIS?

Tennis is a racket sport played between two players or two teams of two players each. Players use rackets to hit a ball over a net into the opponent's court.

HOW MANY PLAYERS PLAY THIS GAME?

Singles: 2 players. Doubles: 4 players.

STRATEGY

Tennis strategy involves serving, returning, court positioning, and exploiting an opponent's weaknesses.

IS IT A TEAM SPORT?

Doubles tennis is a team sport; singles is individual.

RULES OF THE GAME

Points are scored by hitting the ball into the opponent's court within the lines. Sets and matches are won by reaching specific point totals.

IMPORTANT FACTS ABOUT THE GAME

  • Tennis originated in England in the late 19th century.
  • Grand Slam tournaments are the most prestigious events.
  • Tennis is an Olympic sport.

STATISTICS/RECORDS

Records include Grand Slam titles, career wins, and longest matches.

COUNTRIES THAT PLAY THIS GAME

USA
Australia

VOLLEYBALL

WHAT IS VOLLEYBALL?

Volleyball is a team sport where two teams are separated by a net. Each team tries to score points by grounding a ball on the other team's court under organized rules.

HOW MANY PLAYERS PLAY THIS GAME?

Six players per team on the court.

STRATEGY

Volleyball strategy involves serving, setting, spiking, blocking, and digging.

IS IT A TEAM SPORT?

Yes, volleyball is a team sport.

RULES OF THE GAME

Players must hit the ball over the net without touching it more than once consecutively. Points are scored when the ball lands in the opponent's court.

IMPORTANT FACTS ABOUT THE GAME

  • Volleyball was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan.
  • It is an Olympic sport.
  • Beach volleyball is a popular variation.

STATISTICS/RECORDS

Records include tournament wins, individual awards, and match scores.

COUNTRIES THAT PLAY THIS GAME

Brazil
USA
Italy
Poland
Russia

GOLF

WHAT IS GOLF?

Golf is a precision club-and-ball sport, in which competing players use many types of clubs to hit balls into a series of holes on a course using the fewest number of strokes.

HOW MANY PLAYERS PLAY THIS GAME?

Golf can be played individually or in teams of up to four.

STRATEGY

Golf strategy includes club selection, course management, and reading greens.

IS IT A TEAM SPORT?

Golf can be played as both an individual and a team sport.

RULES OF THE GAME

Players must hit the ball into each hole in the fewest strokes possible. Penalties are given for rule violations.

IMPORTANT FACTS ABOUT THE GAME

  • Golf originated in Scotland in the 15th century.
  • The Masters, the US Open, the Open Championship, and the PGA Championship are major tournaments.
  • Golf is an Olympic sport.

STATISTICS/RECORDS

Records include major championship wins, career earnings, and lowest scores.

COUNTRIES THAT PLAY THIS GAME

USA
Scotland
England
Australia
South Korea

CHESS

WHAT IS CHESS?

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on a checkered board with 64 squares arranged in an 8×8 grid. It is one of the world's most popular board games.

HOW MANY PLAYERS PLAY THIS GAME?

Two players.

STRATEGY

Chess strategy involves planning moves, controlling the center of the board, and anticipating an opponent's tactics.

IS IT A TEAM SPORT?

No, chess is an individual sport.

RULES OF THE GAME

Players move pieces according to specific rules, aiming to checkmate the opponent's king.

IMPORTANT FACTS ABOUT THE GAME

  • Chess originated in India in the 6th century.
  • The World Chess Championship determines the world champion.
  • Chess is recognized as a sport by the International Olympic Committee.

STATISTICS/RECORDS

Records include world championship titles, Elo ratings, and tournament wins.

COUNTRIES THAT PLAY THIS GAME

Russia
India
USA
Norway

BILLIARDS

WHAT IS BILLIARDS?

Billiards is a cue sport played on a felt-covered table in which players use a cue stick to strike billiard balls, moving them around a table.

HOW MANY PLAYERS PLAY THIS GAME?

Billiards can be played by two players or in teams.

STRATEGY

Billiards strategy involves aiming, positioning, and planning shots.

IS IT a TEAM SPORT?

Billiards can be played as either an individual or team sport depending on the format.

RULES OF THE GAME

Players use a cue to hit balls, aiming to pocket them or perform specific shots according to the game's rules.

IMPORTANT FACTS ABOUT THE GAME

  • Billiards has various forms, including pool, snooker, and carom billiards.
  • It requires skill, precision, and strategy.
  • Professional tournaments are held worldwide.

STATISTICS/RECORDS

Records include tournament wins, high breaks, and career earnings.

Swimming can be individual or relay (team).
